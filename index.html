Description

This paper presents a case study on Indonesian political volunteerism for President Joko Widodo in the 2014 general election. Description and analysis is given on the nature and complexity of the relationship between independent candidates who later on receive support offer or be nominated by political parties, independence and integrity of Joko Widodo as expected by the public and his supporters, whether political volunteers being rational in supporting candidates of their choice and the criteria of making such judgment as well as functions and strategy Joko Widodo’s political volunteers take to influence public policymaking in his administration.
